What Is Supported Living?

Supported living is a person-centred approach to providing care that focuses on enabling individuals to Live as independently as possible in their own homes, with tailored support to meet their unique needs and aspirations.

This service is designed for individuals who may require assistance due to disability, age, illness, or other Life circumstances, but who value their independence and wish to maintain it. With supported Living, individuals have the chance to Lead fulfilling Lives within their communities, making their own choices and decisions about how they Live, with a helping hand from skilled care professionals when needed.

Reasons To Choose Supported Living

Supported Living is an excellent care choice for individuals seeking to retain independence while receiving necessary support in a safe, community-focused environment. Here's why:

1. Maintain Independence

Supported Living is all about empowerment. It allows individuals to Lead their own lives, make their own decisions, and partake in the activities they enjoy, with a support network in place when needed.

2. Customised Support

Support is personalised to meet the unique needs of each individual. Whether it's help with daily tasks, medication management, or attending social. events, the leveL of assistance adapts to changing needs over time.

3. Community Integration

Supported Living promotes active participation within the community, encouraging individuals to engage in Local activities and events. This social. inclusion fosters a sense of belonging and improves mental. weLL-being.

4. Safe Environment

Living in a supported setting ensures safety and security, with staff available 24/7 for emergencies or just a reassuring presence. This can provide peace of mind for both individuals and their loved ones.

5. Financial Control

Individuals in Supported Living often have more control. over their finances, as costs can be more predictable and manageable compared to other care settings. They can budget for their personal expenses while their care needs are taken care of.

6. Continuity Of Care

Supported Living offers consistency, with familiar faces providing care and support. This builds trust, enhances the quality of care, and strengthens the bond between individuals and their care team.

Our Domiciliary Care Services


  • Personal Care

    We offer support with personal care tasks such as bathing, dressing, toileting, and grooming. Our caregivers respect your privacy and dignity while ensuring your personal hygiene needs are met.

  • Companionship

    Loneliness can be challenging, but our caregivers offer friendly companionship, engaging in conversations, hobbies, or outings, helping to enrich your life with meaningful interactions.

  • Night Care

    We provide overnight care services to ensure your safety and well-being throughout the night. Whether you need assistance with bedtime routines, medication, or reassurance during the night, our caregivers are there for you.

  • Live-In Care

    Our live-in care service provides round-the-clock support, ensuring you receive the help you need whenever you need it. This is a perfect solution for those needing continuous care and wish to remain at home.

  • Catheter Care

    Our team is trained in managing and providing care for individuals with catheters, ensuring proper hygiene and monitoring to prevent infections.

  • Meal Preparation and Diet Support

    Proper nutrition is essential for health and wellbeing. Our caregivers can assist with meal preparation, following dietary guidelines, and ensuring you enjoy a nutritious diet.

  • Household Tasks

    Keeping your home clean and organised can enhance your quality of life. Our caregivers can help with light housekeeping tasks like laundry, dusting, vacuuming, and more.

  • Stoma Care

    Living with a stoma can be challenging, but our caregivers can help manage your stoma care routine, giving you peace of mind and promoting your independence.

  • Medication Assistance

    We can support with medication administration, ensuring you take the right medicine at the right time, contributing to your overall health and wellbeing.

  • Personalised Care

    Unlike residential care, domiciliary care offers a highly personalised service, tailored to the unique needs and routines of each individual. This means the care you receive is always designed around your specific requirements, lifestyle, and preferences.

  • Maintain Independence

    One of the primary goals of domiciliary care is to promote independence. Our caregivers support you with daily tasks and personal care, but also encourage you to do as much as you can for yourself, helping to maintain your independence and self-esteem.

  • Flexible and Adaptable

    Domiciliary care services can be adapted to evolving needs. Whether you need a few hours of support each week or round-the-clock live-in care, services can be adjusted to ensure you receive the right Level of care at the right time.

  • Companionship and Emotional Support

    Domiciliary care is not just about practical support. Our caregivers also provide companionship and emotional support, helping to combat feelings of loneliness and isolation.

  • Peace of Mind for Loved Ones

    For family members, knowing that their loved one is receiving professional care in the comfort of their own home can bring immense peace of mind. You can rest assured that your loved one's needs are being met in a caring and respectful manner.

  • Cost-Effective Solution

    Compared to residential care, domiciliary care can be a more cost-effective solution. You pay for the care you need, which can be adapted and scaled up or down as requirements change.

  • Supports Physical Health

    Besides assisting with personal care and daily tasks, our caregivers can also aid in the administration of medication, nutrition planning, and exercise routines, helping maintain optimal physical health.

  • Helps with Housekeeping

    Our care providers can assist with light household chores such as cleaning, laundry, and meal preparation, ensuring a clean, organised, and comfortable living environment.

  • Continuity of Care

    With domiciliary care, a strong relationship is developed between the caregiver and the client. This continuity of care ensures that the caregiver knows the client's needs and preferences intimately, Leading to a higher standard of care.

  • Retain Local Connections

    Staying at home means maintaining Links with friends, neighbours, and the local community. This social connection plays a vital role in mental and emotional wellbeing.

  • Pet-Friendly

    Pets often provide significant companionship and emotional support. With domiciliary care, you or your loved ones can continue to live with and care for pets.

  • Safeguards Family Relationships

    By alleviating the pressure of care, domiciliary care can help maintain healthy and positive family relationships. It enables family members to spend quality time with their Loved ones, instead of solely focusing on caregiving.

  • Less Disruptive

    For people living with dementia or other cognitive impairments, changing living environments can be highly disruptive and distressing. Domiciliary care minimises this disruption by providing care in the familiar setting of their own home.
